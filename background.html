Inspiration for Greeneville WALKS

Dr. Mary R. Griffin

Anyone who knows me knows how much I love the study of plants; Botany. I do not always get to teach this fascinating subject or share my enjoyment of it as much as I would like, but I keep trying! Before completing my doctorate, I worked as a high school science teacher at Sevier County High School and helped to facilitate an onsite, one-mile nature trail with two rustic, outdoor classrooms. The onsite nature trail I facilitated certified as an arboretum. My peers and I were especially happy about this success because this effort was the first high school-based arboretum project in the state! I was fortunate enough to observe, firsthand, how the project benefited my student’s learning experience. Overtime, the trail became more popular, and the school’s learning environment was greatly enhanced by a sense of shared community. After the positive experience I gained as a high school teacher, I began work at the postsecondary level in education. I welcomed the opportunity to build on a botanical course which examined the impact plants have on our daily lives. While many people recognize the value of vegetation in terms of oxygen creation, lumber, clothing, and food, unfortunately the impact plants and trees have on a region’s culinary choices, medicine, art, music, literature, recreation, and history often go unnoticed. This botanical course allowed me to further explore the overlooked impact of flora with my students and overtime, students, both locally and globally, began to volunteer their knowledge and personal experience. This shared learning enriched the course further, and not only encouraged me in further studies, but encourages me still. The concept of this project is the culmination of life interest and experience.
